Budget Justification

UltraTech AI is not seeking funding to begin building. The platform is built.

Funding is requested to accelerate the structured commercial validation that converts technical readiness into measured SME impact and recurring revenue. Grant funding is the bridge between platform completion and commercial sustainability.

Pilot Programme Delivery — 40%
Recruitment and onboarding of 10–20 UK SMEs. Participant support during 3–6 month pilot. Weekly surveys, exit interviews, and case study production. Without this, commercial validation cannot occur.
Product Completion — 25%
AI Receptionist and Financial Visibility to pilot-ready state. FineGuard is already pilot-ready. Completion work only — the framework is built. Replaceable engine architecture reduces integration cost.
Measurement & Evaluation — 15%
Grant funders require credible measured evidence. Produces publishable data on time saved, compliance improvements, and satisfaction. Evaluation report is a permanent, reusable asset for all future applications.
Commercial Launch Preparation — 10%
Pricing validation, partner packs for banks/accountants/white-label, sales materials from case studies. Ensures UltraTech AI can immediately convert learning into revenue when the pilot ends.

What grant funding does NOT pay for

Ongoing platform maintenance · Business-as-usual operating costs beyond the pilot · Large-scale marketing spend · Equity-style returns or profit distribution. The grant funds a time-bound, outcome-specific programme: the commercial validation of an already-built platform.

1. ARPU is the most powerful lever
£5 increase in ARPU → +£20k Year 2 net and 4–6 months earlier break-even. Tested during pilot exit interviews.
2. Churn is the silent killer
At 5% monthly churn, the subscriber base erodes faster than acquisition replaces it. Retention investment is critical.
3. Partner revenue: high impact, low cost
Even modest partner success significantly improves financial position without increasing direct acquisition spend.
4. Base Case is robust
Only a combination of pessimistic variables pushes break-even beyond 24 months. Any single adverse variable is absorbable.

The grant-funded pilot is designed to reduce uncertainty on the most critical variable — ARPU and willingness to pay — before significant commercial investment is made. The sensitivity analysis confirms the Base Case is achievable and the business has levers to respond if key assumptions move against it.
