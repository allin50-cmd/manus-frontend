# UltraTech AI Budget Justification

Version: 1.0  
Status: Grant-ready  
Derived from: Master Business Plan, Pilot Programme Plan, Commercial Validation Programme

---

## Purpose

To justify the grant funding requested by demonstrating that every pound spent directly contributes to the commercial validation of UltraTech AI with UK SMEs, and that costs are realistic, necessary, and proportionate.

---

## Funding Principle

UltraTech AI is not seeking funding to begin building. The platform is built. Funding is requested to accelerate the structured commercial validation that converts technical readiness into measured SME impact and recurring revenue.

Grant funding is the bridge between platform completion and commercial sustainability.

## Category Justification

### 1. Pilot Programme Delivery (~40%)

**What this funds:**

- Recruitment and onboarding of 10–20 UK SME participants.
- Participant support during the 3–6 month pilot.
- Weekly survey administration and analysis.
- Exit interviews and case study production.
- Participant data export and offboarding.

**Why it is necessary:**

Without structured pilot delivery, commercial validation cannot occur. This is the core activity the grant enables. Every participant generates the evidence — time savings, compliance improvements, willingness to pay — that proves commercial viability.

**Why the cost is realistic:**

Pilot costs are driven by direct participant engagement (modest incentives, support time) and the production of case studies and evaluation reports. No expensive hardware, no large team. Costs are proportionate to a 10–20 business cohort.

---

### 2. Product Completion for Pilot (~25%)

**What this funds:**

- AI Receptionist completion to pilot-ready state.
- Financial Visibility dashboard completion to pilot-ready state.
- Mobile-first refinements based on early participant feedback.
- Any minor integration work required for pilot deployment.

**Why it is necessary:**

FineGuard is pilot-ready. AI Receptionist and Financial Visibility require completion before they can be included in the pilot. Without these services, the pilot tests only one product, reducing the breadth of evidence and the commercial proposition.

**Why the cost is realistic:**

The framework is already built. This is completion work on specific services, not platform construction. The replaceable engine architecture means AI and payment integrations are swappable, reducing integration cost and risk.

---

### 3. Productivity Measurement and Evaluation (~15%)

**What this funds:**

- Design and operation of the measurement framework.
- Weekly survey data collection and analysis.
- Platform analytics instrumentation and reporting.
- Independent evaluation report suitable for publication and future grant applications.

**Why it is necessary:**

Grant funders and partners require credible, measured evidence, not anecdotal claims. This category ensures the pilot produces publishable data on administrative time saved, compliance improvements, and user satisfaction. The evaluation report becomes a reusable asset for all future funding applications.

**Why the cost is realistic:**

Measurement uses a combination of automated platform analytics (low marginal cost) and lightweight weekly surveys. The primary cost is analysis time and report production, not expensive external evaluation.

---

### 4. Commercial Launch Preparation (~10%)

**What this funds:**

- Pricing validation based on pilot data.
- Partner pack creation for banks, accountants, and white-label prospects.
- Sales and marketing materials derived from pilot case studies.
- Grant office maintenance for follow-on funding applications.

**Why it is necessary:**

The pilot's purpose is to enable commercial launch. This category ensures that when the pilot ends, UltraTech AI can immediately convert learning into revenue — through direct subscriptions, partner distribution, and further grant applications.

**Why the cost is realistic:**

This funds preparation activities, not large-scale marketing spend. Materials are derived from pilot evidence that already exists. The Grant Office is a lightweight, process-driven function, not a full-time role.

---

### 5. Contingency (~10%)

**What this funds:**

- Unforeseen technical issues during pilot.
- Additional participant recruitment if dropout exceeds expectations.
- Minor scope adjustments based on mid-pilot review.
- Any compliance or regulatory costs that emerge.

**Why it is necessary:**

A contingency demonstrates prudent financial management to funders. It ensures that unexpected events do not derail the entire programme.

**Why the cost is realistic:**

10% is a standard, conservative contingency for a programme of this scale. It is not a slush fund; it is a managed reserve drawn only with justification.

---

## What Grant Funding Does Not Pay For

Grant funding is not requested for:

- Ongoing platform maintenance (covered by future subscription revenue).
- Business-as-usual operating costs beyond the pilot period.
- Marketing spend beyond pilot recruitment and commercial preparation.
- Equity-style returns or profit distribution.

The grant funds a time-bound, outcome-specific programme: the commercial validation of an already-built platform.
