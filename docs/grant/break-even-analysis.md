Version: 1.0  
Derived from: Commercial Assumptions v1.0, Three-Year Financial Model

---

## Monthly Operating Costs (Steady State, Post-Pilot)

| Cost Category | Monthly (£) |
|---------------|------------|
| Infrastructure & hosting | 700 |
| AI/voice API usage | 500 |
| Customer support & ops | 1,500 |
| Sales & marketing | 1,000 |
| Professional services | 300 |
| Team (founder + ops) | 3,000 |
| **Total monthly costs** | **£7,000** |

Note: This assumes post-pilot, grant-funded activities have concluded. Team costs may increase with growth, but are offset by higher revenue.

## Break-even Timeline (Base Case: £25 ARPU, 280 subscribers)

| Milestone | Subscribers | Month (approx.) |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| Pilot complete | 0 (paid) | Month 6 |
| First paying subscribers | 10–15 | Month 7 |
| Early growth | 50 | Month 12 |
| Approaching break-even | 200 | Month 18 |
| Break-even | 280 | Month 20–24 |

---

## Key Insights

- Break-even is achievable within 24 months on the Base Case, funded initially by grant support and then by customer revenue.
- The primary driver of break-even speed is ARPU. Bundled subscriptions and partner revenue accelerate the timeline.
- Infrastructure costs grow sub-linearly; the marginal cost per additional subscriber is low. The business becomes more profitable as it scales.
- If ARPU reaches £30 (bundled Professional plan) and partner revenue contributes, break-even could occur as early as Month 16–18.
