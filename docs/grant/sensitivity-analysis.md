# UltraTech AI Sensitivity Analysis

Version: 1.0  
Derived from: Three-Year Financial Model (Base Case), Commercial Assumptions v1.0

---

## Purpose

To demonstrate how the Base Case financial model responds to changes in key assumptions, and to identify the variables that most affect break-even and sustainability.

---

## Variables Tested

| Variable | Base Case | Pessimistic | Optimistic |
|----------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| ARPU (£/month) | 25 | 20 | 30 |
| Pilot-to-paid conversion | 30% | 20% | 50% |
| Monthly churn | 3% | 5% | 2% |
| Customer acquisition cost (direct) | £80 | £120 | £50 |
| Partner revenue contribution (Year 2) | £15,000 | £5,000 | £30,000 |
| Infrastructure cost growth (relative to customer growth) | 0.5x | 0.7x | 0.3x |

---

## Impact on Year 2 Net Position (All Costs)

| Scenario | Year 2 Net (£) | Break-even Month (approx.) |
|----------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Base Case | -5,400 | 20–24 |
| Pessimistic ARPU (£20) + higher churn (5%) | -28,000 | 30+ |
| Optimistic ARPU (£30) + higher conversion (50%) | +22,000 | 14–16 |
| Pessimistic conversion (20%) + higher CAC (£120) | -19,000 | 26–30 |
| Optimistic partner revenue (£30k) + lower churn (2%) | +31,000 | 12–14 |
| Pessimistic infrastructure (0.7x) + low ARPU (£20) | -24,000 | 28–32 |
| Optimistic all variables | +52,000 | 10–12 |

---

## Key Findings

1. **ARPU is the most powerful lever.** A £5 increase in ARPU improves Year 2 net by ~£20,000 and pulls break-even forward by 4–6 months.
2. **Churn is the silent killer.** At 5% monthly churn, the subscriber base erodes faster than acquisition can replace it. Retention investment (bundles, service quality) is critical.
3. **Partner revenue is a high-impact, low-cost accelerator.** Even modest partner success significantly improves financial position without increasing direct acquisition spend.
4. **Infrastructure costs are not a major risk factor.** The shared framework architecture keeps cost growth sub-linear even in pessimistic scenarios.
5. **The Base Case is robust.** Only a combination of pessimistic variables (low ARPU + high churn + poor conversion) pushes break-even beyond 24 months. Any single adverse variable is absorbable.

---

## Mitigations Built into the Model

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| ARPU below target | Pricing validated during pilot; bundles offer clear upgrade path; premium services (AI Receptionist) command higher willingness to pay. |
| Churn above target | Multi-service bundles increase stickiness; permanent business records create switching costs; customer feedback loop enables rapid service improvement. |
| Conversion below target | Pilot designed to capture willingness to pay before launch; extended free access for case study/referral participants; pricing adjusted based on exit interview data. |
| Partner revenue delayed | Direct-to-SME channel remains viable independently; no dependency on any single partner. |

---

The sensitivity analysis confirms that the Base Case financial model is achievable and that the business has levers to respond if key assumptions move against it. The grant-funded pilot is designed to reduce uncertainty on the most critical variable — ARPU and willingness to pay — before significant commercial investment is made.
